★ Deep Insights & Practitioner Takeaways

1. Morning IV inflates L3 by ~85% — you'd be defensive twice as often.
At 3:30 PM close, 82 days (11.0%) are L3. At 9:15 AM, 152 days (20.3%) are L3. Morning VIX/IV is structurally elevated due to overnight risk premium and opening auction volatility. If you classify regimes using morning IV, you'll treat 70 extra days as "crisis mode" when they're actually moderate-vol days that settle down by close. This is a 9.3pp false-alarm rate on L3.
2. Close snapshots produce cleaner quintile gradients.
At 3:29/3:30, L1 quintiles show a clean monotonic AL% pattern: Q1 (1.5%) → Q5 (13.4%). At 9:15/9:16, the pattern breaks — Q3/Q4/Q5 jump around. This is because morning PK/IV ratios are computed with inflated IV denominators, compressing the range and reducing signal quality. PK/IV ratio has higher discriminating power when computed from close IV.
3. L2 Caution-B changes character entirely between close and morning.
• Close (3:30): AL% = 14.5%, Sharpe = 0.46, DM Sharpe = -2.41, WC Sharpe = -1.41 → genuinely dangerous state
• Morning (9:15): AL% = 6.3%, Sharpe = 3.31, DM Sharpe = 0.71, WC Sharpe = 3.77 → looks like a safe state!
The population of days classified as "L2 + low PK/IV + rising IV" is completely different depending on when you measure IV. The close-based Caution-B captures true risk events. The morning-based Caution-B captures days where IV is temporarily elevated but PK/IV is still low — those days have great outcomes. The close interpretation is the real one.
4. The Safe/Exposed AL% gap is robust across all 4 snapshots.
Despite all the noise, the core insight holds everywhere:
• L1: Safe 3.3-4.8% vs Exposed 9.3-10.2% — 5-6pp gap always present
• L2: Safe 4.2-6.7% vs Risky 7.8-12.5% — 3-6pp gap always present
• L3: Safe 7.3-11.8% vs Exposed 17.1-22.4% — 8-12pp gap always present
PK/IV separates risk at every snapshot. The magnitude changes, but the direction never flips.
5. Orion collapses in L3 at morning snapshots.
L3 Safe Orion Sharpe: +2.44 (3:30) → -1.16 (9:15) → -1.29 (9:16). L3 Exposed Orion Sharpe: -1.90 (3:30) → -1.97 (9:15). Orion's directional bets get destroyed on days with elevated morning IV that aren't truly high-vol days. If using morning snapshots, avoid Orion in L3 entirely. DM is the only strategy with consistent L3 Safe performance (4.48, 4.48, 2.63, 2.49 across all snapshots).
6. VRP rank ordering is the most snapshot-invariant signal.
L3 Safe always has the highest VRP (4.76-5.09) and L1 Exposed always has the lowest (1.27-1.76) across ALL 4 snapshots. The top and bottom never flip. When your regime says "Safe" and VRP is high, trade with maximum conviction — this is the one signal that doesn't care when you measured IV.
7. Morning snapshots break the L2 tested-and-failed conclusions.
At 3:30, adding IV direction to L2 adds 5.3pp of AL% separation. At 9:15, it actually HURTS by -1.4pp. At 9:16, adding IV direction to L1 improves performance (which contradicts the close-based finding). The regime construction methodology was designed around close IV and should only be used with close IV. Morning IV introduces enough noise that some of the carefully validated relationships reverse.
8. Recommendation: Use 3:30 T-1 as the canonical snapshot.
• It uses the official close — settlement, margin, institutional flow all anchor to this
• Produces the cleanest quintile gradients (monotonic AL% in L1)
• L3 population is compact (82 days) and discriminating (Safe Sharpe 5.49 vs Exposed -0.21)
• Tested-and-failed conclusions are internally consistent
• All strategies behave as expected (WC strong in L2 Safe, DM strong in L3 Safe)
• Morning snapshots (9:15, 9:16) are useful for intraday regime awareness ("is IV elevated this morning?") but NOT for regime classification and strategy selection
9. When could morning snapshots ADD value?
• Early warning: If 9:15 says L3 but 3:30 T-1 said L2, today might be transitioning to higher vol — tighten stops
• Mean reversion signal: If 9:15 IV is 5+ points above yesterday's 3:30 close IV, history says it'll come down — this is a VRP opportunity
• Opening trade timing: Use the morning snapshot to decide WHEN to enter, but use the close snapshot to decide WHAT to trade and HOW much size
These are separate use cases from regime classification — don't mix them.
